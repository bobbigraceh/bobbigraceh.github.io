Blue Lights Effect On Health

Raise your hand if you turn your phones brightness down at night and turn it up in the morning. Well majority of us do because that’s how our bodies are trained. When the suns up it’s a sign to wake up, and when the moon’s up it’s a sign to call it a night. Typically, after you’re done watching Netflix, you turn down your brightness when doing your last scroll through social media or finishing up your last texts of the day. Though in the morning when you’re ready to wake up and start another day you increase your brightness. Why is this if you can still see your screen even when it’s turned down? It’s because that’s how our bodies are naturally trained. Light indicates our bodies to wake up, and naturally without even thinking about it we turn our brightness up to wake us up.

Our brightness does so much more than just wake us up though. Our phone light stimulates us which keeps us more aware and awake. This affects the quality of our eyes as well as our sleep; including our anxiety and depression levels as well as our mood swings. These are all due to the blue light being emitted from your screen. I believe companies such as Apple, Windows, Androids, etc. should regulate the amount of blue light emitted during the late hours of the night in order to better our overall health in the long run.

Now you might be against that because it should be up to us if we want to destroy our mental well-being, right? Well what if this was implemented when screens were first produced? You would be used to the tent by now. But it’s a little too late for us to implement it and not realize the difference now. Does that mean it’s too late to save ourselves from the effects though? There are still many ways to lessen the effects that are in our control.

One way to lessen those is to either wear blue light glasses or download blue light filtering software. Though these have side effects such as giving off an orange display, which many of y’all are familiar with since it’s the same tent night display mode gives off. And in addition to the tent, there is also a decrease in quality. Now these are things to turn you away from using night mode, protective glasses, or screens, understandably. But how important are those when it comes to your health?

            Think about it. There is a reason why those funky blue light prevention glasses exist, and it’s quite obvious. They’re to protect your eyes!

 

 Blue Light Affects Your Eyes:

Ying Zhou in 2018 published a research on Progress About the Effect and Prevention of Blue Light on Eyes which concluded the blue light emitted can penetrate through your Retina which is your initial site of vision formation. It also passes straight through your cornea which shoots a powerful ray of light to the back of your eyes stimulating your brain and altering your hormonal balance. This directly correlates to the quality of sleep you can get.

 

Blue Light Affects Sleep:

Cause and Effect: Harvard Medical School in 2018 published a study on Blue Light Has A Dark Side which concluded the blue light emitted by our phone screen is the most powerful when it comes to suppressing the amount of melatonin we produce. To clarify, melatonin is also called the “sleep hormone” so the higher your melatonin levels the faster you fall asleep. Basically saying the more you expose yourself to blue light, by looking at your phone screen, the less melatonin your body will produce therefor making it harder to sleep.

 

Now I know what you’re thinking, okay we’re all college students here. What even is sleep, right? Considering I sleep maybe 3 hours on a good night I’m probably not the person to speak on this, but sleep deprivation can actually cause serious effects on our health.

 

 

Effects of Sleep Deprivation:

Alice M. Gregory in 2012 published a clinical review on Sleep, Emotional and Behavioral Difficulties in Children and Adolescents in which stated that anxiety, depression, attention disorders, and effects on our emotional and behavioral levels result from a normal disrupted sleep pattern. A normal sleep pattern doesn’t necessarily mean 8 hours a night, because honestly who has time for that much sleep now a day. But it’s saying what’s your daily routine at night. Personally, I shower then sleep. So, once I shower my body knows my day’s about to be over so it produces the “sleep hormone”, melatonin that we talked about earlier. So, if I decide to shower then study it’s not only not going to be efficient but it’s also messing with my sleep schedule and throwing off my levels. My mind will not know when it’s time to call it a day therefor it won’t produce enough melatonin for me to actually get quality sleep out of it.

You could be saying to just take more melatonin then. Rebecca Costello in 2014 published a journal on The Effectiveness of Melatonin For Promoting Healthy Sleep: A Rapid Evidence Assessment of The Literature that melatonin cannot perform a positive benefit on sleep efficiency. You could take melatonin to fall asleep, and as there isn’t a high chance of becoming dependent on it there are still side effects. Brent Bauer in 2017 published an article on Pros and Cons of Melatonin in which he listed the side effects consisted of headaches, dizziness, nausea, and drowsiness.

If you remember from earlier when I stated the effects of blue light, those were also headaches, dizziness, nausea, and drowsiness. Now everyone take out your phones. Now everyone in here took it out immediately without even thinking about protecting themselves or turning on night mode.

 

 

And as soon as you turned your phone on, the blue light being emitted from your screen was sent through your cornea.

And within the time I took to say that, those light rays passed straight through your eyes slowly causing an increase in your brain activity.

Now your brain is stimulated from just a minute of looking at your screen.

Your brain is now awake and active. How do you expect to fall asleep when your brain is jumping off the walls? You won’t. Now you might not regret taking out your phone right now, but tonight when you’re lying in your bed waiting to fall asleep you’ll regret freely looking at your screen without protection. This is why I believe companies such as Apple, Window, Android, and many more. should reduce the blue light emitted from our screens during a certain period of the night to increase our overall health in the long run.
